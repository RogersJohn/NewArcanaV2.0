# **Overview**

In New Arcana, players take on the role of Prince Electors of the Holy
Roman Empire. Upon the death of the Emperor, these kings, dukes and
bishops; all representing powerful dynasties within the Empire, hold
sway over the selection of the new successor.

The Emperor is known to be unwell and already political machinations are
in play across the empire to gain as much influence and power as
possible ahead of the vote. Will you succeed against your competing
princes?

New Arcana is a competitive tableau-building card game for 2 to 6
players. It uses a customised deck of cards based on the Tarot, some of
the earliest playing cards ever invented. Players will compete over
several rounds to gain influence, represented by victory points, until
the Death card is drawn, signifying the passing of the Emperor.

Traditionally, the Tarot is split into two groups of cards: the Minor
Arcana of 56 cards and the Major Arcana of 22 cards. Over several turns,
players will build a tableau of up to 5 Minor Arcana, creating poker
combinations. The highest ranking hand will win the pot of victory
points for the round, but the stakes are always increasing, with more
victory points added to the pot in each subsequent round.

The 22 Major Arcana cards may be purchased to score bonuses and gain
asymmetric advantages over your opponents, but the cards also represent
the sands of time passing for the sickly Emperor. Soon the Death card
will appear, but exactly when, no one can tell - or can they? Perhaps
some shrewd and observant players will gain insight from the Tarot!

The winner will be the player with the most influence, but will that be
the mundane influence of politics and power, represented by victory
points, or the celestial influence of the fates as decreed by the cards?

The player with the most victory points will win when the Death card is
revealed. But all may not be as it seems with an alternate win
condition! If you possess any three of the powerful Celestial cards at
the end of any round, you are granted immediate victory, denying your
opponents, and Death, their expected triumph.

But do you have what it takes?

# Set Up

-   Set out the 3 game boards on the table as pictured

-   Give each player one player reference mat

-   Place 1vp token per player in the middle of the table - this is the
    > initial pot of points that players will be competing over

-   Place the Round-End Trigger marker beside the pot of victory points

-   Randomly decide the first dealer, and give them the Dealer token

-   If playing with 2-5 players, use the first 22 Major Arcana cards,
    > numbered from 0 to 21 at the top of each card and put the others
    > back in the box. With 6 players, use all 26 Major Arcana cards.

-   Separate the cards into two piles; one pile of 56 Minor Arcana Cards
    > (the suit cards from Ace to King) and the Major Arcana cards

-   Place the ***Death*** card (number 13) face up on the table

-   The Dealer shuffles both decks and deals each player 5 Minor Arcana
    > cards and 2 Major Arcana Cards

-   Players select 1 Major Arcana card to keep, placing the other face
    > up on the table beside the ***Death*** card

-   Create the Major Arcana draw pile, with ***Death*** randomly placed
    > in the bottom 3 cards by following these steps:

1.  Take the face-up Major Arcana cards, except ***Death***, and shuffle
    > them

2.  Deal out these cards face down onto the table until there are only
    > two left in hand

3.  Place the dealt cards at the bottom of the Major Arcana pile

4.  Take the ***Death*** card and shuffle it well with the remaining 2
    > cards

5.  Place these final 3 cards at the bottom of the Major Arcana pile

-   Place the Major Arcana draw pile into its designated location on the
    > game board

-   Draw the top 3 Major Arcana cards face up and place them into the 3
    > locations on the Major Arcana Display

-   Place the Minor Arcana draw pile on its designated location and turn
    > the first card face up beside it, forming the Minor Discard pile

-   The player to the left of the Dealer becomes the Active Player and
    > takes their first turn

# Game Flow

The game is played over a series of rounds and each round is made up of
a number of player turns.

During their turns players are trying to build a tableau of 5 cards as a
poker combination. See the section at the end on poker hand rankings if
you are not familiar with them. If you ever start your turn with 5 cards
in your tableau, also called your **Realm**, the round is over, and the
player with the highest ranking poker combination in their Realm wins.
There are more details on this in the Round-End section.

## Turn Structure

Start of Turn: Declare round-end if you have 5 cards in your Realm - see
Round-End instructions below

If the round has not ended, then complete your turn:

1.  Draw up to 6 cards - see Draw Phase and handsize rules below

2.  Buy or Play (both are optional)

3.  Discard down to 6 cards if needed

Turn End: Play moves to the left with the next player becoming the
active player.

## Draw Phase

When calculating "handsize" in New Arcana, the number of cards in your
hand **plus** the number of cards in your Realm are added together.

So if you have 3 cards in your Realm, and 1 card in your hand you have 4
cards.

When drawing, you draw up to 6 cards, or take just 1 card if you have 6
or more cards. So in the above example you would draw 2 cards to your
hand.

**Remember, even if you start with 6 or more cards, you still always
draw at least 1 card.**

## Play Phase

In this optional phase you have three choices;

1.  Play a "set" to your Realm

2.  Play a Royal attack card

3.  Play a Major Arcana card to you Tome or for its Action

### Playing To The Realm

You can think of adding cards to your Realm as carrying out political,
military or commercial actions to strengthen your own principality and
influence.

Cards played to your Realm must be played as one of the allowed sets:

-   A single card

-   A Pair: 2 cards of the same value

-   Three-of-a-Kind: 3 cards of the same value

-   A Straight: 5 cards of consecutive values, but in different suits
    > (for example 2,3,4,5,6 of any suit)

-   A Flush: 5 cards of the same suit, but not in sequence

-   Four-of-a-Kind: 4 cards of the same value

-   A Straight Flush: 5 cards of consecutive values of the same suit

-   Five-of-a-Kind: 5 cards of the same value (only possible with a
    > "wild" card)

Poker players will notice that 2 poker combinations, Two-Pair and
Full-House, are not listed as allowed sets. These are still possible
winning hands in New Arcana, but are considered to be two sets each for
the purposes of playing cards into your Realm.

**Only a single complete set, or cards that will complete/repair one
existing set, may be played into your Realm.**

It can happen that you want to play a set into your Realm that would
bring the number of cards passed 5. For instance, if you had played 3
twos in your first turn, followed by 3 tens in your second turn. This is
allowed. Simply move on to the Discard Phase.

It is also possible to play a "wild" card into your Realm, either on its
own or as part of a set. See the Wild Card rule section below.

When you have finished playing cards into your Realm, move to the
Discard Phase.

### Attack Play

The Minor Arcana have one more Royal card per suit than the standard
Poker deck. These are Page, Knight, Queen and King. The normal Poker
deck replaces the Page and Knight with just the Jack. These four Royal
cards, along with the Aces, can be played into your Realm to make
winning combinations, but they can also be used for special actions.

Each of these cards has **Action** text, showing that they can be played
for a special attack action if needed.

A single Action card can be played on your turn, following these steps:

1.  Play the card, following the text on the card

2.  Announce the intended target

3.  Place the card face-down in the Pit

The Pit is a special discard pile beside the Minor Draw pile. These
cards are always placed face down and cannot be used again during the
current round. If ever the Minor Arcana draw pile runs out, the Minor
Arcana discard pile is reshuffled to form a new draw pile, but cards in
the Pit are never recycled in this way. This is to ensure Action cards
are only ever used once for their action during a given round.

See the Card Actions section below to see details of how each card
works.

### Major Arcana Plays

Major Arcana cards can be used in a number of ways. Some will give end
of round bonuses, some give ongoing benefits or abilities, some can be
used for special attacks and all of them can be used as "wild" cards
when trying to build combinations in your Realm.

For the purposes of bonuses and ongoing benefits, Major Arcana cards are
played onto the table into a separate area. You can think of this as a
mini-tableau that holds a maximum of 3 cards. This second tableau is
referred to as your **Tome**, and represents influence with important
and powerful figures, fostering loyalty within your principality, behind
the scenes plots, conspiracies, spying, sabotage and perhaps even
supernatural interventions.

Cards that are played to your Tome have the word **Tome** written in
bold at the top of their text box. Tome cards remain in place between
rounds, and can deliver benefits for the rest of the game.

However, they can also be the target of attacks by some special Major
Arcana cards.

Lastly, whilst the Tome can only hold 3 cards, it is possible to play
more cards into your Tome. In this case, simply choose one of the
original 3 cards and discard it into the Pit.

You can only have a maximum of 3 active Tome cards at one time.

### Bonus Cards

Some Major Arcana cards list end-of-round bonuses and are marked
**Bonus - Round End**.

***Justice***, for example, will award you 1vp at the end of the round
if you have the highest number of Sword cards in your Realm, regardless
of whether you won the round or not.

[Remember, bonus cards only trigger if you have at least 1 card in your
Realm.]{.underline}

So to earn the bonus point from ***Justice*** you would need at least
one card in your Realm, and your Realm would have the highest number of
Sword cards above zero. That means if another player has more Sword
cards than you, you don't earn the point, however you can tie for the
highest number of Swords and still get the point.

Compare this to ***The Magician***, another bonus card. This card allows
you to select the suit of cards you will earn your bonus from, but the
text on the card says "most" cards. That is, you must have more cards
than any other opponent.

### Other Tome Cards

Some Tome cards offer benefits that aren't simple bonuses. One of the
abilities of ***Temperance***, for instance, is to protect Cups cards in
your Realm from attack. ***The Devil*** increases your handsize by one
card. These cards and benefits persist between rounds as long as you
keep those cards in your Tome. Again, these cards carry the **Tome**
keyword at the top of their text box.

## Buy Phase

Instead of playing cards you may wish to purchase one Major Arcana card.

This is achieved by spending up to 3 cards from your hand. Once you
complete your purchase move on to the Discard Phase and Turn End.

### Buying Cards

You may buy a single card from either the top of the Major Arcana Draw
pile, the Display or the top of the Major Discard pile by discarding [up
to 3 cards]{.underline} from your hand to the Minor Discard pile.

This diagram shows the tMajor Arcana Draw Pile, Display and Discard
Pile. The numbers at each position represent the price of each card. To

To buy the face-up card on top of the Major Arcana Discard pile costs

These prices are paid by adding the values of cards you will discard to
the Minor Arcana Discard pile.

To buy a card in the leftmost position of the Display, you could discard
a 9, or perhaps a 5 and a 4, or even a 2, 3 and a 5. You are allowed to
"over pay", but can only spend a maximum of [3 cards]{.underline}.

In some rare cases, some players may even want to spend a Royal card or
a Major Arcana as part of a purchase. This is allowed, and in these
cases, the value paid is equal to the number printed at the top of the
Major Arcana card.

Royal cards are worth 11, 12, 13 and 14 respectively for Page, Knight,
Queen and King cards.

The purchased card is taken into your hand. If the card was taken from
the second or third display slot, slide any cards left-to-right into the
empty spaces, as indicated by the arrows, then refill the Display by
turning the topmost card from the draw pile face-up into the first
display slot.

Then proceed to the Discard Phase.

## Discard Phase

In the Discard Phase you count the total cards between your hand and
Realm. If the number is greater than 6, discard cards from your hand
until the total equals 6.

In some unusual cases, it can occur that you have more than 5 cards in
your Realm. If that is the case, first discard cards from your Realm in
your desired order to the Minor Arcana Discard pile, then count and
discard cards from your hand as normal.

Note that cards are discarded to the Minor Discard Pile even if
discarding a Major Arcana card.

## Turn End

At this point check to see if you have five cards in your Realm. If you
do, you **must** announce that you have triggered round-end and check to
see if anyone else has the Round-End Marker. If not, take possession of
the marker and place it in front of you at the table.

Either way, play passes to the player on your left, who is now the
Active Player.

If you have the round-end marker when your next turn starts, Round-End
will be triggered. Just follow the rules in that section.

It will often happen that one or more cards will be removed from your
Realm before play returns to you. In that case, give the Round-End
marker to the first player on your left with 5 cards in their Realm. If
no one has 5 cards, the Round-End marker is returned to the center of
the table beside the pot.

## Round End

As stated before, if you start your turn with 5 cards already in your
Realm, then Round-End is declared. Round-End can also be declared due to
a card effect (for example ***Death*** or ***Judgement***). Either way,
complete the following steps:

1.  Check if a player has the round-end marker. If so, someone will win
    > the pot of victory points, go to step 2. If not, the pot will not
    > be awarded - this may happen if ***Death*** has just been
    > revealed.

2.  Check all Realms to see who has the strongest poker combination.
    > Remember, it is not necessary to have 5 cards in your Realm to
    > win. For instance, a player with only three 2s in their Realm will
    > beat another player who has Two-Pair and a fifth card. The player
    > with the highest ranking poker combination wins the pot.

3.  Then, starting with the winning player, each player assesses any
    > bonus cards they may have. These will often offer 1 or 2 victory
    > points if the conditions on the card are met. Remember, [bonus
    > cards only trigger if you have at least 1 card in your
    > Realm]{.underline}. If the round ends before you've played any
    > cards, none of your bonus cards will trigger.

4.  The Major Arcana Display is "aged". That is, the card in slot 3 is
    > placed face up on the Major Arcana Discard pile next to it, the
    > other 2 cards are each moved one place to the right, and a new
    > card is turned up from the draw pile and placed in the leftmost
    > slot of the Display.

5.  Gather up all Realm cards, the Minor Arcana draw pile, discard pile
    > and the Pit, and shuffle well with the deal passing to the left.

6.  Place into the pot a number of victory points from the supply equal
    > to the last round's points plus 1.

7.  Now deal 6 cards to each player from this deck. Note, it is normal
    > for some Major Arcana cards to pass into the Minor Arcana Drawl
    > pile over time.

## Game End

The end of the game may be triggered for the following reasons:

-   The ***Death*** card is drawn or revealed

-   A player ends a round with 3 or more ***Celestial*** cards

-   Not enough Minor Arcana cards are left to draw or deal, even after
    > the Minor Arcana Discard pile has been shuffled to refill the draw
    > pile

When this happens do the following:

1.  If a player has the round-end marker, award the Pot to the player
    > with the best Realm. In the unusual case that all Realms are
    > empty, the Pot is not awarded.

2.  Award round-end bonuses to players who have cards in their Realms
    > and meet their bonus cards' requirements

3.  Award end-of-game bonuses to players who have end-of-game bonus
    > cards and meet their requirements

Note, toward the end of the game, ***Death*** may be revealed between
rounds as the Display is being refilled. As no one has any cards in play
the pot is not awarded and no points are scored for that round - move
directly to end-of-game scoring.

Otherwise, the game can end suddenly mid-round if a card is purchased or
cards run out.

Either way, add up all the points and the player with the most points
wins. In the event of a tie, the player with the highest value Tome card
wins, using the number at the top of the Major Arcana cards. If neither
tied player has a Tome card, players share the victory.

# Detailed Card Rules

## Wild Cards

Besides being played for attacks or bonuses, Major Arcana cards may also
be played into your Realm as a "wild" card. Wild cards are a concept
borrowed from poker where certain cards may be given any value or suit
by the player using them. The Joker is commonly used for this purpose,
allowing a player to have an extra Queen, for instance, or fill in for a
missing card in a Straight or Flush.

In New Arcarna, any Major Arcana Card may be played into your Realm as a
wild card if there are currently no other Major Arcana cards in your
Realm.

When playing a wild Major Arcana card like this you announce the rank
and suit you want assigned to the card in order to identify the set you
are playing to your Realm.

Once in the Realm, the wild card can be thought of as being of every
suit and any value all at once, and is assumed to be the card that would
make the strongest possible poker combination from the cards available
in the Realm at the time.

However, whilst wild cards are very powerful, they are also very
vulnerable.

As they are being played into the Realm, another player may choose to
block the wild card by playing an Ace (see the section on Aces below).
If the wild card is blocked it is placed in the Pit along with the Ace
that blocked it. Any Minor Arcana cards being played with the wild card
when it is blocked are still placed in your Realm.

Also because wild cards are considered to be every suit at once, a wild
card in your Realm may be the target of any attacking Royal card of any
suit.

A Major Arcana card can be identified as a Royal card if being played
into your Realm (as a third Queen, for example), however a Major Arcana
card cannot be identified as a Royal card for the purpose of playing a
Royal card for its Action. They cannot be used for Royal attacks.

Note, even though you may not play a wild card into your Realm if one is
already there, it is possible to have more than one wild card in your
Realm. Queens, for instance, move cards between Realms, and may attack
wild cards.

## Action Cards

### Minor Arcana Action Cards

Here's what each card does.

### Page

A Page of a given suit may attack a card of the same suit in any Realm,
discarding the target card to the Pit. So both the Page and the target
card are moved to the Pit.

### Knight

A Knight of a given suit may attack a card of the same suit in any
Realm. In this case the card is taken into the attacking player's hand.
The Knight is placed in the Pit.

### Queen

A Queen of a given suit may attack a card of the same suit in an
opponent's Realm. In this case the card is moved directly into the
attacking player's Realm. The Queen is placed in the pit.

### King

The King is a little unusual in that it is a defense card that is played
in response to an attack, when it is not your turn. If another player
attacks your Realm with a Page, Knight or Queen, you may immediately
show a King of **any** suit, to block the attack. When this happens both
the King and the attacking card are placed in the Pit. Note, you may
only use a King to defend your own Realm.

### Aces

Aces are the most powerful card in the game.

Aces may be played at any time during a round, even out of turn.

Aces are blocking cards and can be used to block any Royal action,
meaning an attack by a Page, Knight or Queen, or the blocking action of
a King. Aces can even block an Ace being played by another player.

This means a player with an Ace can affect a conflict between two other
players.

Lastly, Aces can be used to block Major Arcana cards being played from
another player's hand - see the section on Major Arcana cards. Aces have
no effect on cards that have successfully "landed" in your Realm or
Tome.

### Major Arcana Action Cards

Some Major Arcana cards are action cards. They may be played for their
effect and then placed in the Pit, just like Royal Minor Arcana cards.
They are marked with the keyword **Action**.

Some of these, like ***Strength***, ***The Hanged Man***, and ***The
Tower*** are attack cards, and they target cards in the Tomes of other
players.

Other Action cards like ***The Wheel of Fortune*** and ***Judgement***
perform specific actions within the game that should benefit any player
that can use them wisely. Simply follow the text on the cards.

### Celestial Cards 

Four of the Major Arcana are referred to as ***Celestial*** cards, and
carry the **Celestial** keyword at the top of their text box. Note, a
fifth is added if playing with 6 players.

Celestial cards do not provide any end-of-round bonus if held in your
Tome, but do carry a bonus if you hold the cards in either your Realm or
Tome at the end of the game.

More importantly, if you have at least 3 of these cards in your Tome
and/or Realm at the end of any round, you win the game immediately,
ignoring any victory points held by any players.

### [You now know everything you need to play your first game.]{.underline}

We recommend you play a test game to get used to the rules and various
cards.

# Variants

The following variants are suggested if you would like to vary the game.

## Ace High

In New Arcana the Ace has the value 1 and is lower than a 2. So a pair
of Aces would lose to a pair of 3s, for instance. Aces are very powerful
blocking cards in New Arcana, so it seems fitting that they are very
weak when played into a Realm, and are worth very little when purchasing
cards.

Many poker players will find this jarring, as they are used to Aces
being the strongest card, with the strongest Straight being 10, J, Q, K,
A. In poker the Ace is the value 1, lower than a 2 [and]{.underline}
higher than a King all at the same time.

If you want to play the Ace like this in New Arcana, you can do so. It
won't have a huge effect on the game and if you enjoy playing the Aces
like that, why not. Just make sure everyone is in agreement on how Aces
are used before you begin play.

## Two-Player New Arcana

If playing with two players, it is suggested you remove all cards of
value 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6. If playing this variation please remember that
the lowest run is 7, 8, 9, 10, Page. You will probably want to play this
in conjunction with the Ace-High variant.

Also note that in this variant the Flush is a very difficult combination
to achieve and so the hand rankings are changed in the following manner:

-   Five-of-a-Kind

-   Straight Flush

-   Flush

-   Four-of-Kind

-   Full House

-   Straight

-   Three-of-a-Kind

-   Pair

-   High Card

## Six Player New Arcana

New Arcana is played with 22 Major Arcana cards when played with up to 5
players. These cards are valued from zero (***The Fool**)* to 21 (***The
World***).

If playing with 6 players, add in the four extra supplied Major Arcana
cards with values from 22 to 26. This will ensure you have a long enough
game.

## Extended Arcana

If you get bored of seeing the same Major Arcana in the 2 - 5 player
game, why not shuffle the extra Arcana used for 6 players into the Major
Arcana deck during set-up and then randomly remove 5 cards from the game
without looking at them?

## The Vault

In this variant, the first time you win a round, you take your winning
Realm and place it under your player mat - these cards are now called
your Vault. They are not shuffled back into the deck for the next round.
Vault cards stay out of the game and only you may look at them to check
their values. It is deemed good etiquette to confirm the rank of your
Valt if asked however. "Three-of-a-Kind" or "Two-Pair" should be
sufficient for most players.

If you win a subsequent round, check to see if your new winning Realm
represents a better hand than your Vault. If it does, then replace your
Vault, releasing those old cards to be shuffled back in for the next
deal.

As the game proceeds, and winning players take cards into their Vaults,
a number of cards will become unavailable. Remembering what cards are
not available is important when assessing what sets are possible and
what attack cards or Major Arcana are in play.

At the end of the game, the player with the best Vault hand earns
victory points equal to the number of players in the game. Vault hands
are also used as a tie breaker instead of consulting Tome cards.

Celestial cards are deemed to be in your possession if in your Realm,
Tome or Vault, so you now have a secure third location in which to hide
those valuable treasures.

Note, this variant cannot be played with 6 players as too many cards are
taken out of circulation for the game to work properly.

## Charity

Another suggested rule variant is to allow any player who did not score
any points in a single round to carry one card from their hand, [if they
have any]{.underline}, into the next round.

The extra card will have to be discarded during their first Discard
phase, but this allows players who may have no options left at the end
of a round to begin planning for the next - even buying a Major Arcana
card with a view to playing it later.

# Poker Hand Rankings

Listed strongest to weakest in descending order.

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Hand Name**     **Description**
  ----------------- ---------------------------------------------------------
  Five of a Kind    Five cards of the same rank - only possible with a wild
                    card

  Straight Flush    Five consecutive cards of the same suit

  Four of a Kind    Four cards of the same rank

  Full House        Three-of-a-Kind plus a Pair

  Flush             Five cards of the same suit, but not consecutive

  Straight          Five consecutive cards of mixed suits

  Three-of-a-Kind   Three cards of the same rank

  Two Pair          Two different pairs

  One Pair          Two cards of the same rank

  High Card         No matching cards; highest card determines the winner
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
