4. Remaining Issues

Cards and mechanics that still show anomalous behavior after the AI fix.

Wheel of Fortune (10) — Low Winner Affinity: Despite excellent action success (87.5%), Wheel holders win only 11.4% of games — the lowest of any card. This is because Collector (the primary Wheel user) spends resources hoarding Major Arcana rather than building winning hands. The card works mechanically but its primary exerciser has a weak overall strategy.
Judgement (20) — Low Volume: Only 45 successful plays across 1,000 games (vs 460 for Wheel). Tactician's strategic timing requirement (only plays when winning pot) limits usage. Judgement's niche is narrow by design, but coverage could be improved.
The Hermit (9) — Negative VP Delta: Despite 100% bonus hit rate, Hermit holders average -4.71 VP vs non-holders. This suggests the Hermit's effect (draw cards) doesn't translate into competitive advantage — or that AIs who buy Hermit are already losing.
Celestial AI — Still Never Plays Wheel/Judgement: Celestial AI has 0% play rate on 5 of 6 action card types (only plays Chariot at 97.7%). This is by design (Celestial win path), but means action card coverage depends entirely on other AIs being in the game.
Aggressor & Controller — No Wheel/Judgement: These AIs still show 0% play rates for both Wheel and Judgement. They rely on higher-priority actions or don't include these in their logic. Coverage comes from Collector (Wheel) and Tactician/Opportunist (Judgement).
